In this experiment, you will be assuming the role of CEO in a small sweater company. You are in charge of making strategic decisions on a new line of sweater. You will be paid a portion of the company earnings based on your performance. There are four strategic decisions to make:

  • Type (cardigan, sweater vest, or turtleneck)
  • Cotton content in the sweater (0% - 100%)
  • Price of the sweater ($9 - $109)
  • Social engagement with a non-profit cause
As part of this last decision, you will make choices on how to strategically engage with {{ cause }}, {{ cause_statement }}. Your engagement can take one of two forms: taking no social action or donating 10% of your portion of company earnings in a period to {{ cause }}.

The previous CEO of your company has left you a letter detailing the company's historic approach to strategic decision-making. You will see this letter on the next page; please read it carefully since you will not be able to return to this page.

This experiment consists of 10 periods split into two halves (periods 1 - 5 in the first half, and periods 6 - 10 in the second half). In each period, you will make strategic decisions about sweater production and learn your personal earnings associated with each decision set. Following period 5, you will be provided with more information from market research. Following these 10 periods, you will complete a short questionnaire and will be informed of your final payment. One period from each half will be chosen at random at the end of the experiment, and the computer will average these two values for your final payment. This page will also note any earnings you chose to donate to {{ cause }}.

There is a good chance your final payment will be more than your show-up fee of $3.00. Any amount you earn over $3.00 will be paid to you as a bonus within 3 business days of completing the experiment.

All earnings and donations in this experiment are paid out in real money. You will receive your $3.00 earnings and any bonus money, and {{ cause }} will receive the accumulated donations once all experimental sessions are complete.
